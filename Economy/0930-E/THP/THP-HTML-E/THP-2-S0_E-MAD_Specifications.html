Ethics Charter Supplementary Document: E-MAD Official Specifications (v1.2 Operable Version)

0. Preamble and General Principles

Preamble This document establishes the official specifications for E-MAD (Effective Multilateral Assured Denial) as a core operational subsystem of The Horizon Protocol (THP). Whereas the Ethics Charter provides an abstract and normative foundation (human decency, atonement, condemnation), this specification prioritizes concreteness and rigor. E-MAD defines how it is measured, executed, and governed, and ensures compatibility with each of THP's charters and operational dashboards.

E-MAD is a non-violent, transparent, and multilateral framework. Its purpose is not retaliation, but to reliably deny the success of aggressors, thereby preventing war and catastrophic escalation.

1. Conceptual Foundation

  1. Human Decency as a Boundary: The line between sins that can be atoned for and sins that should be condemned.

  2. Atonement — Red: Acts of aggression. The subject can return to the international community after atonement and fulfillment of obligations. Function: Restorative and reintegrative.

  3. Condemnation — Crimson: Nuclear attack, crimes against humanity, etc. Non-violent and absolute measures. No forgiveness, and the stigma is permanent. Function: Protective and exclusive.

  4. Principle of Denial of Success: To make the act of aggression itself irrational.

2. E-MAD Hierarchical Response System

Operated on a 5-level scale.

  • Blue (Baseline): Normal state. Continuous monitoring and public education.

  • Yellow (Caution): Early warning. Diplomatic engagement.

  • Orange (Critical Point): Crisis state. Multilateral diplomatic intervention and pressure through non-punitive price signals (risk premium surcharge).

  • Red (Atonement): Act of aggression confirmed. Multifaceted denial-of-success measures are activated, including reparations, risk premiums, access restrictions, compliance investments, and monitoring obligations. A path to social reintegration is shown based on the Atonement Difficulty Index (ADI).

  • Crimson (Condemnation): Nuclear attack or crime against humanity. Absolute and non-violent measures are activated, such as the termination of all debt privileges (one strike and you're out). The perpetrator's appeal is not permitted.

Application to Ethnic Conflicts (Phased Sanctions)

  • Stage 1: Raising of funds, settlements, and insurance rates (targeting the violating entity).

  • Stage 2: Cutting off the pipeline of important materials (excluding medical care and food).

  • Stage 3: Permanent establishment of corridor security by UN-PDF.

  • The ignition condition is linked to the KPI of the People-Charter, and the final judgment is in the name of the Council of Human Decency.

3. Measurement Indicators

  1. E-MAD Score (0–100): A composite indicator that aggregates denial capabilities in multiple areas.

  2. Estimated Loss: Quantifies the losses of the aggressor, victim, and third parties in monetary terms.

  3. Atonement Difficulty Index (ADI, 0–100): A multifaceted indicator that measures the difficulty of social reintegration in Red cases.

  4. Transparency Requirements: All data, models, and code are open-sourced, and independent audits are mandatory.

4. Governance and Process

  1. Tripartite Governance:

    • Expert Committee: Methodology, models, quality control.

    • Citizens' Assembly: Ensuring legitimacy through deliberation.

    • Council of Human Decency: The sole adjudicating body for E-MAD stage decisions.

  2. The Four Sages — Advisory Body:

    • No voting rights. Not a member of the Council of Human Decency.

    • Crimson Cases: Can exercise a one-time right to request reconsideration.

    • Red Cases: Can exercise a one-time right to request a special pardon after substantial progress has been made on the ADI.

    • All opinions are published as public memoranda, and the final decision belongs to the Council of Human Decency.

  3. Due Process, Standard of Proof, and Appeal:

    • Standard of Proof: Red = high probability, Crimson = beyond a reasonable doubt.

    • Appeal: In Crimson cases, the perpetrator's appeal is not possible. In Red cases, it is possible based on the ADI target.

    • Humanitarian Exception (Zero-Stop Principle): Medical care, food, water, and relief communications are never stopped.

Annexes

Annex A — Color Classification Thresholds

E-MAD score range (initial proposal, updated quarterly by public RFC)

  • Blue: ≥ 80

  • Yellow: 65–79

  • Orange: 50–64

  • Red: < 50 (however, immediately shifts to Red with the "execution of aggression/attack, etc." trigger)

  • Crimson: Applied immediately regardless of score when the human decency condemnation trigger (nuclear/genocide, etc.) is activated

Annex B — ADI (Atonement Difficulty Index) Initial Weights

Vectors and weights (total = 1.00)

  • a. Reparation capital: 0.30

  • b. Risk premium coefficient × period: 0.20

  • c. Access restriction period: 0.15

  • d. Compliance verification: 0.15

  • e. Victim relief: 0.15

  • f. Minimization of humanitarian impact: 0.05

Aggregation: Weighted geometric mean of normalized indicators → ADI. Re-entry examination is possible when the ADI is 60 or higher.

Annex C — Risk Premium Grant Formula for Orange

Proposed coefficients: RP = β0 + β1·ESG_risk + β2·SupplyFrag + β3·SanctionProb Updated weekly. β coefficients are determined by public RFC, and the regression formula and dataset are open-sourced.

Annex D — Standard Contract Clauses (Model Clauses)

  • [Crimson Acceleration Clause] "In the event of a Crimson judgment under the E-MAD protocol, with the exception of the humanitarian zero-stop exception, all debt privileges shall terminate and all outstanding debts shall become immediately due and payable. The parties confirm that this decision will be mutually recognized in all jurisdictions in which they participate."

  • [Red Rehabilitation Clause] "Red cases shall be subject to a risk premium surcharge, temporary access restrictions, and compliance investments through a third-party deposit until the ADI threshold is met under independent monitoring."

Annex E — Due Process Forms (Templates)

  • Judgment notification (Orange/Red/Crimson): Summary of facts / summary of evidence / applicable provisions / remedies

  • Set of evidentiary documents: Data source, time stamp, verification code

  • Appeal form (Red only): Reason for dissatisfaction / additional evidence / corrective proposal

  • Reconsideration memorandum (Four Sages): One-time / public opinion paper

Annex F — The Four Sages' Authority Protocol (Distinction)

  • No voting rights. Institutionally separate from the Council of Human Decency.

  • Crimson: Right to request reconsideration (one-time, submission → publication → council deliberation).

  • Red: Right to request a special pardon (one-time, after reaching ADI progress).

  • All are archived as public memoranda.

Annex G — Humanitarian Zero-Stop (Operation of Humanitarian Exceptions)

  • Scope: Medical care, food, water, relief communications, logistics of vaccines/essential medicines.

  • Implementation: Permanent whitelist and third-party audit. Zero stop events are the design target (KPI).

Note on Organizational Alignment (Council / Court / Four Sages)

  • The final judgment is in the name of the Council of Human Decency. The Court of Human Decency is the judicial branch.

  • The Four Sages are advisory (no vote). This specification shall be consistent with THP-ORF / Ethics Charter.
