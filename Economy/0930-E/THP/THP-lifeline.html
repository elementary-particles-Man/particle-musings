The sole objective of this plan is to guarantee the uninterrupted flow of designated lifeline resources hubbed in Japan, India, and Singapore, even after the occurrence of Walpurgis. This will be achieved through the following four interconnected mechanisms.

  • Trade Finance Window: The Export Credit Agencies (ECAs) of Japan, India, and Singapore will jointly re-guarantee trade finance that has become dysfunctional.
  • International Reinsurance Pool: A public-private partnership will underwrite war and political risks that commercial insurance cannot cover, thereby maintaining maritime transport.
  • Fast Track Corridor: A dedicated route for the priority processing of customs clearance, port operations, and financial settlement of lifeline resources.

    * See appendix for HS Whitelist (medical, water, electricity, food, infants). Customs simplification and mandatory eBL for target cargo only.

  • Non-USD Settlement System: To cope with the depletion of USD liquidity, a trilateral settlement system using JPY, INR, and SGD will be enabled.
